# Employment Agreement (Template) --- Northstar Software, Inc.

**Employee:** Emma Johnson\
**Position:** Senior Software Engineer\
**Company:** Northstar Software, Inc.\
**Company Location:** Austin, Texas, USA\
**Effective Date:** January 8, 2024

> **Demo / Template Notice (Not Legal Advice):** This document is a
> sample employment agreement for demonstration purposes only.
> Employment agreements should be reviewed by qualified legal counsel
> and adapted to applicable federal, state, and local laws.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 1. Parties

This Employment Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into by and
between **Northstar Software, Inc.** (the "Company") and **Emma
Johnson** (the "Employee"), effective as of the **Effective Date** set
forth above.

## 2. Position; Start Date; Duties

### 2.1 Position

The Company employs Employee as a **Senior Software Engineer**. Employee
will report to a manager designated by the Company (the "Manager").

### 2.2 Start Date

Employee's employment commenced on **January 8, 2024** (the "Start
Date").

### 2.3 Duties and Responsibilities

Employee will: - Perform software engineering duties consistent with the
Senior Software Engineer role, including designing, developing, testing,
deploying, and maintaining software systems. - Participate in code
reviews, incident response/on-call rotations (if applicable), and
technical planning. - Collaborate with cross-functional teams (product,
design, QA, security, and operations). - Perform other duties reasonably
assigned by the Company that are consistent with Employee's position and
skills.

### 2.4 Work Location

Employee's primary work location is **Austin, Texas**, or such other
location as may be agreed upon by the Company and Employee. The Company
may require reasonable travel as needed for business purposes.

## 3. Employment Relationship (At-Will)

### 3.1 At-Will Employment

Employee's employment with the Company is **at-will**. This means that
either the Company or Employee may terminate the employment relationship
at any time, with or without cause, and with or without notice, subject
to applicable law.

### 3.2 No Contract for a Term

Nothing in this Agreement creates a contract for a specific term of
employment. Any modification of at-will status must be in a written
agreement signed by Employee and the Company's authorized officer.

## 4. Compensation

### 4.1 Base Salary

Employee will be paid a base salary of **USD \$125,000 per year**, less
applicable taxes and withholdings, payable in accordance with the
Company's standard payroll practices.

### 4.2 Performance Reviews and Adjustments

Employee's performance and compensation may be reviewed periodically in
accordance with Company practice. Any salary adjustments are
discretionary and will be determined by the Company.

### 4.3 Business Expenses

The Company will reimburse Employee for reasonable and necessary
business expenses incurred in the performance of duties, in accordance
with Company policy and subject to required documentation and approvals.

## 5. Work Schedule; Timekeeping

### 5.1 Standard Working Hours

Employee's standard work schedule is **Monday through Friday, 9:00 AM to
5:00 PM** local time, with reasonable flexibility as business needs
require.

### 5.2 Exempt Classification (Typical for Senior Engineers)

Employee is expected to be classified as **exempt** under the Fair Labor
Standards Act (FLSA), subject to applicable law and Company
determination. Exempt employees are paid on a salary basis and are
expected to fulfill their responsibilities regardless of specific hours
worked.

## 6. Benefits and Leave

### 6.1 Benefits

Employee will be eligible to participate in the Company's benefit plans
generally offered to similarly situated employees, subject to plan terms
and eligibility requirements. Benefits may include health insurance,
retirement plans, and wellness programs as offered by the Company.

### 6.2 Paid Time Off and Holidays

Employee will be eligible for Company leave and holiday policies as in
effect from time to time. Company policies may be updated at the
Company's discretion, subject to applicable law.

## 7. Confidentiality and Data Security

### 7.1 Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" includes non-public information relating to
the Company's business, products, customers, pricing, finances,
employees, systems, security practices, source code, and trade secrets,
as well as any third-party confidential information entrusted to the
Company.

### 7.2 Non-Disclosure Obligation

Employee agrees to: - Hold all Confidential Information in strict
confidence. - Use Confidential Information solely for Company
business. - Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party
without the Company's prior written consent, except as required by law.

### 7.3 Data Security and Acceptable Use

Employee agrees to comply with Company information security policies,
including: - Using Company-approved devices, accounts, and tools for
Company work. - Maintaining strong passwords and multi-factor
authentication where required. - Promptly reporting suspected security
incidents, data loss, or unauthorized access.

## 8. Company Property

All Company property (including laptops, keys, badges, documents, and
any Company data) remains the Company's property. Upon termination of
employment or upon request, Employee will promptly return all Company
property and delete/return any Company data in Employee's possession,
custody, or control.

## 9. Intellectual Property; Work Product

### 9.1 Work Product

"Work Product" means inventions, discoveries, designs, improvements,
processes, works of authorship, software, documentation, and other
materials created by Employee (alone or with others) during employment
that relate to the Company's business or are created using Company time,
equipment, or resources.

### 9.2 Assignment

To the fullest extent permitted by law, Employee assigns to the Company
all right, title, and interest in and to all Work Product, including all
intellectual property rights.

### 9.3 Further Assurances

Employee agrees to sign documents and take reasonable actions necessary
to secure or confirm the Company's rights in Work Product.

## 10. Conflicts of Interest; Outside Activities

Employee agrees to avoid conflicts of interest and to disclose any
potential conflicts promptly. Employee will not engage in outside
employment or consulting that materially interferes with job
responsibilities or competes with the Company, without prior written
approval, subject to applicable law.

## 11. Policies; Code of Conduct

Employee agrees to comply with all Company policies and standards of
conduct as may be updated from time to time. Company policies are
guidelines and do not create a contract.

## 12. Termination; Final Pay

Upon termination of employment, Employee will receive final pay in
accordance with applicable law and Company policy, including any accrued
but unused paid time off if required by applicable law or policy.

## 13. Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the **State of Texas**,
without regard to conflict-of-law principles, except to the extent
federal law or mandatory local law governs.

## 14. General Provisions

### 14.1 Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties
regarding Employee's employment and supersedes prior discussions or
representations.

### 14.2 Amendments

Any amendment to this Agreement must be in writing and signed by both
parties.

### 14.3 Severability

If any provision is held unenforceable, the remaining provisions will
remain in effect.

### 14.4 Electronic Signatures

Signatures may be executed electronically and will be deemed binding.
