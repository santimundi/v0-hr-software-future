## 1. Overview

This policy covers paid time off (PTO), sick leave, holidays, and other
leave types. Local laws and individual employment contracts may provide
additional rights or different requirements.

**Principles:** - Time off supports health, productivity, and
sustainability - We plan ahead to maintain coverage - Access is fair,
consistent, and auditable

## 2. Annual Leave / PTO

### 2.1 Eligibility

All full-time employees accrue annual PTO. Part-time employees accrue
pro-rata.

### 2.2 Entitlement & Accrual (Example)

-   **20 days per calendar year** for full-time employees
-   Accrues at **1.67 days per month**
-   Accrual begins on the employee's start date

### 2.3 Carry Over

Up to **5 days** may be carried over to the next year. Carried-over days
must be used by **March 31** unless otherwise required by local law.

### 2.4 Request Process

1.  Submit PTO requests at least **2 weeks** in advance for planned
    leave
2.  Manager approval is required
3.  HR approval required for **\>10 consecutive business days** (example
    control)

Requests should include: - Date range - Any partial days - Coverage plan
(handoff notes, point of contact)

### 2.5 Blackout / Peak Periods

During high-demand periods (e.g., fiscal year-end, major releases),
leave may require additional review. Where possible, teams should
publish blackout periods in advance.

### 2.6 Partial Days & Hourly Conversion

Teams may track PTO in days or hours. If tracked in hours, PTO is
deducted based on the employee's standard daily hours.

## 3. Sick Leave

### 3.1 Entitlement (Example)

Employees receive **10 days** sick leave per year. Unused sick leave
does not carry over unless required by local law.

### 3.2 Notification

Employees should notify their manager before the scheduled start time on
the first day of absence (or as soon as practical).

### 3.3 Documentation

A medical note may be required for absences exceeding **3 consecutive
workdays** (subject to local law). Employees should not share detailed
diagnoses unless necessary; HR can facilitate privacy-preserving
documentation.

## 4. Personal Days (Example)

Employees may have **2 personal days** per year for urgent matters that
don't fit PTO or sick leave categories. Personal days: - Are requested
through the same system as PTO - Typically do not carry over

## 5. Holidays

The company observes paid holidays based on the employee's work
location. A holiday calendar is published annually. If business needs
require working on a holiday, managers will coordinate alternative time
off where permitted.

## 6. Parental Leave (Example)

Eligible employees who become parents through birth, adoption, or foster
placement may receive paid parental leave. Details vary by jurisdiction.
Example framework: - Primary caregiver: up to 12 weeks paid - Secondary
caregiver: up to 6 weeks paid

Employees should notify HR as early as possible to coordinate leave,
benefits, and payroll.

## 7. Bereavement Leave (Example)

Employees may take bereavement leave in the event of a family member's
death. Example: - 3 days paid for immediate family - Additional unpaid
leave may be approved

## 8. Jury Duty / Civic Leave

Employees may take leave for jury duty or civic obligations per local
law. Provide documentation when available. The company will not
retaliate for lawful civic participation.

## 9. Unpaid Leave & Special Leave

Employees may request unpaid leave for: - Extended travel - Personal or
family reasons - Education or sabbatical (where offered)

Approval depends on role, performance, and business needs and must be
documented.

## 10. Leave Tracking, Approvals & Auditing

### 10.1 System of Record

All leave should be requested and approved through the HRIS/time-off
system. Email approvals should be avoided unless the system is
unavailable.

### 10.2 Approval Expectations

Managers should consider: - Policy eligibility and notice period - Team
coverage - Ongoing deadlines or operational risk

### 10.3 Audit & Access Controls

Leave data is personal data. Access is role-based: - Employees can view
their own leave - Managers can view direct reports' leave - HR can view
for legitimate HR purposes All access is logged.

## 11. Misuse & Corrective Action

Examples of misuse: - Repeated last-minute absences without notice -
Misrepresentation of leave - Working while on medical leave in violation
of guidelines

Misuse may lead to corrective action.

## Appendix A: Manager Coverage Planning (Guidance)

When evaluating a request: 1. Validate entitlement/balance 2. Review
overlap with other leave 3. Identify coverage risks (on-call, customer
commitments) 4. Propose alternatives if necessary (split leave,
different dates, partial coverage)

## Appendix B: Employee FAQs

**Q: Can I take PTO if I'm out of sick leave?**\
A: In some cases yes, subject to manager approval and local law.

**Q: What happens if I leave the company?**\
A: PTO payout rules vary by jurisdiction and policy. HR will provide an
exit statement.

**Q: Can my manager deny leave?**\
A: Managers can deny or propose alternatives to maintain business
continuity, but should do so fairly and consistently.
