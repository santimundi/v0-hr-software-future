## 1. Overview

This guide explains benefit options at **Northstar Software, Inc.**,
eligibility rules, enrollment windows, and how to make changes. Benefit
availability varies by location and role.

**Benefits typically include:** - Medical, dental, and vision
insurance - Life and disability insurance - Retirement plan (e.g.,
401(k) or equivalent) - Health savings / flexible spending accounts
(HSA/FSA) where applicable - Wellness stipend and employee assistance
resources (EAP) - Optional benefits (commuter, supplemental coverage)

## 2. Eligibility

An employee is eligible for benefits if: - They are in an eligible
employment category (e.g., full-time) - They meet any waiting period
requirements (if applicable) - They reside in a location where the
benefit is offered

**Eligible dependents** may include: - Legal spouse or domestic partner
(where allowed) - Children up to a specified age (commonly 26),
including adopted children

Documentation may be required (marriage certificate, birth certificate,
etc.).

## 3. Enrollment Windows

### 3.1 New Hire Enrollment

New hires typically must enroll within **30 days** of start date. If you
miss the window, you may have to wait until Open Enrollment unless you
have a Qualifying Life Event (QLE).

### 3.2 Open Enrollment

Open Enrollment occurs annually (example: November). During this period,
employees can: - Add or drop coverage - Change plan types - Update
dependents - Adjust FSA/HSA contributions

### 3.3 Qualifying Life Events (QLE)

A QLE allows you to change benefits outside Open Enrollment. Examples: -
Marriage/divorce - Birth/adoption - Loss of other coverage - Relocation
to a new coverage area

Changes must typically be made within **30 days** of the event.

## 4. Medical Coverage (Example Options)

Common plan types: - **PPO:** More provider flexibility, higher
premiums - **HMO:** Lower premiums, network restrictions, PCP
referrals - **HDHP + HSA:** Higher deductible, lower premiums, HSA
eligibility

Key terms: - **Premium:** Amount you pay per paycheck/month -
**Deductible:** Amount you pay before insurance pays - **Copay:** Fixed
cost per visit - **Coinsurance:** Percentage share after deductible -
**Out-of-pocket max:** Maximum you pay in a year (covered services)

### Choosing a Plan (Simple Guide)

-   Choose **PPO** if you want flexibility and frequent care
-   Choose **HMO** if you want lower premiums and your providers are
    in-network
-   Choose **HDHP+HSA** if you want lower premiums, are generally
    healthy, and want tax-advantaged savings

## 5. Dental and Vision (Example)

Dental plans often cover: - Preventive care (cleanings) - Basic services
(fillings) - Major services (crowns), possibly with a waiting period

Vision plans often cover: - Annual eye exam - Frames/lenses allowance -
Contacts allowance

## 6. Life and Disability Insurance (Example)

### 6.1 Life Insurance

Company-paid life insurance may be provided (example: 1--2x annual
salary). Supplemental life may be available for employees and
dependents.

### 6.2 Short-Term and Long-Term Disability

Disability insurance replaces income during qualifying illness/injury.
Terms vary; consult plan documents for waiting periods and benefit
percentages.

## 7. Retirement Plan (Example: 401(k))

The company may provide: - Employee contributions via payroll - Company
match (example: 4% match) - Vesting schedule (example: vests over 3
years)

Employees can: - Set contribution percentages - Choose investment
options - Update beneficiaries

## 8. Wellness & Stipends (Example)

Example offering: - **\$500 annual wellness stipend** for gym
memberships, fitness equipment, classes, or wellness programs

Employees should submit receipts through the reimbursement portal per
policy.

## 9. How to Enroll (Step-by-Step)

1.  Log into the Benefits Portal
2.  Confirm personal information and dependents
3.  Select medical/dental/vision plans
4.  Choose voluntary benefits (if applicable)
5.  Enter HSA/FSA contribution amounts (if applicable)
6.  Review costs per paycheck
7.  Confirm beneficiaries
8.  Submit and save confirmation

## 10. Common Pitfalls (Avoid These)

-   Missing the enrollment window
-   Forgetting to add dependents
-   Not designating beneficiaries
-   Selecting an FSA amount you can't justify (FSA may have "use it or
    lose it" rules)
-   Assuming your doctor is in-network (verify!)

## 11. Support

If you need help: - Contact HR Benefits Support - Contact the insurance
provider support line - Ask your manager for scheduling flexibility to
complete enrollment

## Appendix A: Glossary

**Coinsurance:** Your percentage responsibility after deductible\
**Deductible:** Amount you pay before plan coverage begins\
**EOB:** Explanation of Benefits statement from insurer\
**In-network:** Providers contracted with insurer at negotiated rates\
**Premium:** Cost deducted from paycheck to maintain coverage

## Appendix B: FAQs

**Q: Can I waive coverage?**\
A: Yes, if you have other coverage. You may need to provide proof.

**Q: If I add a dependent later, can I update benefits?**\
A: Only during Open Enrollment or after a QLE.

**Q: What happens if I leave the company?**\
A: Depending on location, you may be eligible for continuation coverage
(e.g., COBRA in the US) or other arrangements.
