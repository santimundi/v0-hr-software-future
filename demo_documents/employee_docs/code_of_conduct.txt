## 1. Purpose

The Code of Conduct explains expectations for ethical behavior,
professionalism, and compliance at **Northstar Software, Inc.**. It
applies to all employees, contractors, and interns.

## 2. Guiding Principles

-   Act with integrity and good judgment
-   Treat others with respect
-   Protect company and customer data
-   Comply with laws and policies
-   Speak up when something seems wrong

## 3. Respectful Workplace

We do not tolerate discrimination, harassment, bullying, or retaliation.

Examples of unacceptable conduct: - Derogatory comments or slurs -
Sexual harassment or unwelcome advances - Threats, intimidation, or
repeated hostility - Excluding others in ways that harm their work

Managers have elevated responsibilities to model respectful behavior and
to respond promptly to concerns.

## 4. Equal Opportunity & Inclusion

We support equal opportunity and strive to maintain diverse teams.
Employment decisions must be based on business needs and merit.

## 5. Conflicts of Interest

Avoid conflicts and disclose potential conflicts, including: - Working
for a competitor - Personal relationships that affect reporting lines -
Vendor relationships involving family/friends - Outside business
interests that intersect with company work

When in doubt, disclose.

## 6. Gifts, Entertainment, and Anti-Bribery

Employees must not: - Offer, promise, or accept bribes - Provide gifts
that create an appearance of improper influence - Accept cash or cash
equivalents

Modest business meals may be acceptable if lawful and appropriately
documented.

## 7. Confidentiality & Company Property

Protect confidential information, including: - Customer data and
contracts - Product roadmaps and code - Security reports and incident
details - HR data (salary, performance, personal details)

Company property includes devices, credentials, and intellectual
property created in the course of employment.

## 8. Security & Data Privacy

### 8.1 Access Control

Use least privilege. Do not attempt to access data you are not
authorized to view. Access is monitored and logged.

### 8.2 Safe Systems Use

-   Use approved tools and platforms
-   Keep systems patched
-   Report suspected phishing or incidents immediately

### 8.3 AI Tools

Do not share confidential or personal data with non-approved AI tools.
Use approved workflows and follow AI safety settings.

## 9. Financial Integrity & Records

Accurate recordkeeping is required. Do not: - Falsify expense reports -
Manipulate performance metrics - Misrepresent time worked or time off

## 10. Workplace Health & Safety

Follow safety procedures and report hazards. Violence, threats, or
weapons (where prohibited) are not allowed.

## 11. Reporting Concerns (Speak Up)

Report concerns promptly via: - Manager - HR - Compliance/ethics
channel - Anonymous reporting where available

Non-retaliation is strictly enforced.

## 12. Investigations and Corrective Action

The company may investigate reported issues and take action including
coaching, warnings, performance plans, or termination. Serious
violations may lead to immediate termination.

## Appendix A: Examples (Quick Reference)

**Okay:** - Declining a gift politely - Reporting a suspected data leak
immediately - Giving respectful feedback in private

**Not okay:** - Looking up coworkers' salaries "out of curiosity" -
Sharing customer data in a public Slack channel - Retaliating against
someone who reported misconduct

## Appendix B: FAQs

**Q: Can I talk about my compensation?**\
A: Local laws vary; in many jurisdictions employees have rights to
discuss pay. Still, do not disclose other people's pay or confidential
HR records.

**Q: What if my manager is involved in the issue?**\
A: Report to HR or use an alternative reporting channel.
