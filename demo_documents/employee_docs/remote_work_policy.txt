## 1. Purpose & Scope

This policy defines how remote and hybrid work operates at **Northstar
Software, Inc.**. It applies to employees in roles eligible for remote
work, subject to manager approval and business needs. Some roles (e.g.,
facilities, certain support functions) may require on-site presence.

Remote work is a work arrangement---not a benefit or entitlement. The
company may modify remote arrangements as team needs change.

## 2. Definitions

-   **Remote work:** Work performed off-site (home or another approved
    location).
-   **Hybrid work:** Combination of on-site and remote work.
-   **Core hours:** Time window when employees should be generally
    available for collaboration (unless otherwise agreed).

## 3. Eligibility

Employees are generally eligible if they: - Have completed probation (or
an equivalent introductory period) - Meet performance and reliability
expectations - Have a role that can be performed effectively off-site -
Maintain an appropriate work environment (see Section 6)

Managers may deny or modify remote schedules based on: - Customer
needs - Security requirements - Team collaboration requirements -
Performance or conduct concerns

## 4. Remote/Hybrid Schedule

### 4.1 Standard Guideline

A common arrangement is up to **3 days per week** remote, with manager
approval. Teams may adopt different schedules.

### 4.2 Core Hours

Employees should be available during core hours such as **10:00--16:00
local time** (team-defined). Work outside core hours may be flexible,
but should not create sustained collaboration gaps.

### 4.3 Location Constraints

Remote work must be performed from an approved location. Employees
must: - Provide an address for emergency and tax compliance purposes
(where required) - Notify HR/manager of changes to primary work
location - Obtain written approval before working from a different
country/state if it impacts tax, payroll, or compliance

## 5. Performance & Communication Expectations

Remote work succeeds when expectations are explicit:

-   **Availability:** Maintain agreed working hours and respond within
    reasonable timeframes.
-   **Meetings:** Join on time, camera use as team norms require, and
    stay engaged.
-   **Documentation:** Prefer written updates in shared channels
    (tickets, docs) over private messages.
-   **Output:** Performance is evaluated on results, not visible
    activity.

Recommended team norms: - Daily async updates (e.g., "what I'm doing
today / blockers") - Calendar hygiene (keep availability updated) -
Meeting discipline (agendas, outcomes, action items)

## 6. Workspace Requirements & Safety

Employees must maintain a safe, professional workspace: - Reliable
internet and phone access (as role requires) - A quiet environment
suitable for calls - A dedicated workspace that protects confidential
information - Ergonomic setup (chair, monitor height, lighting)

Employees should: - Take breaks and maintain healthy work habits -
Report work-related injuries or incidents promptly

## 7. Equipment, IT Support & Expenses

### 7.1 Company-Provided Equipment

Depending on role, the company may provide: - Laptop and accessories -
Security key or MFA device - Standard peripherals (mouse, headset)

All equipment remains company property.

### 7.2 Software and Updates

Employees must: - Install security updates promptly - Use
company-approved software - Not disable endpoint protection

### 7.3 Expense Reimbursement (Example Policy)

Remote work expenses may be reimbursed if pre-approved: - Internet
stipend (if offered) - Ergonomic equipment (with receipts) - Travel to
company offices when required

The company does not reimburse discretionary home office upgrades unless
pre-approved.

## 8. Information Security & Privacy

### 8.1 Data Handling

-   Do not print sensitive information unless required and approved
-   Store confidential documents securely
-   Lock screens when away
-   Use VPN where required

### 8.2 Public Wi-Fi

Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive work. If unavoidable, use: - VPN -
Tethering - Secure hotspots

### 8.3 Recording & Monitoring

The company may monitor systems for security and compliance. This is not
intended for micromanagement; it supports safety and legal obligations.

## 9. Meetings & On-Site Requirements

Managers may require periodic on-site attendance for: - Team planning
sessions - Customer visits - Security-sensitive work - Training or
onboarding

Employees should plan for reasonable travel when necessary. Travel
policies apply.

## 10. Exceptions & Reviews

Remote arrangements should be reviewed periodically. Exceptions may be
granted for: - Medical accommodations - Family needs - Temporary
relocation requests

All exceptions must be documented and approved by HR/leadership as
needed.

## 11. Policy Violations

Failure to comply may result in: - Adjustment or removal of remote work
privileges - Corrective action under the Code of Conduct - Security
incident procedures (if data is compromised)

## Appendix: Remote Work Checklist

**Before going remote:** - Confirm schedule and core hours - Test
connectivity and audio/video - Ensure MFA is working - Review data
handling rules

**Weekly hygiene:** - Keep calendar updated - Post status updates -
Document decisions and action items - Confirm next week's collaboration
needs

## FAQs

**Q: Can I work remotely while traveling?**\
A: Short domestic travel may be permitted with manager approval, subject
to security and time zone constraints. International travel requires
additional approvals due to tax/compliance.

**Q: What if my internet goes out?**\
A: Notify your manager immediately and use backup options (hotspot or
alternative location) if feasible.

**Q: Can remote workers be promoted?**\
A: Yes. Promotions are based on impact, not location.
